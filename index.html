This test is meant to be taken on behalf of your current or previous partner. Taking the test for yourself has proven to be wildly inaccurate, so we advice against it. You can, however, take the test on behalf of someone else's partner (friends are a great vibe check).

The Red Flag Project was created to educate and promote healthy relationships. At the core of this project is the Red Flag Test. This test was created to validate people's experiences, identify problems that can be worked on, and give people a visual representation of the things they don't deserve to tolerate in relationships.

Now obviously all red flags are not treated the same; wearing flip flops in a non-aquatic setting is NOT the same as punching holes in walls. Originally our ranking of red flags was kinda arbitrary & based heavily on our opinions and experiences. To make this as legit as possible, I consulted therapists, relationship experts, and surveyed young adults to validate the ranking process.

You may not agree with some of the red flags on this list. Cry about it :/ But actually, this tool is not meant to determine if your partner is abusive. It's meant to identify areas that can be improved or to call attention to behaviors that you don't deserve to tolerate. It's also meant to be fun, so yea there are stupid red flags on here, but they're ranked accordingly and its not that deep.

Trigger Warning: Some Red Flags are often seen as precursors or symptoms of abuse, or relating to sexual assault. These topics could be triggering to survivors. Take care of yourself !

Results

Grade: A+++ Good for you! Everyone's always got a few red flags, but your partner's doing pretty well! The bar is on the floor, but they're above the floor. They respect you and treat you like a human being.

Grade: B Not bad! There might be a few problems, but nothing that can't be worked out with some good communication!

Grade: C Not the best to be honest. There are some serious issues that need to be addressed. It's going to take more than communication; you need to see significant change in your partner.

Grade: D Well ... I wish I could say that things will get better. Things aren't looking good. It will take a significant amount of time and a lot of emotional labor on your part to teach your partner how to be a good spouse. Not worth imo, but that's up to you.

Grade: F Oof :/ This is rough. The worst grade they could get. If this is your ex, I hope you feel validated that they were in fact a piece of shit. If this is your current partner, you deserve better.

Says Slurs

A slur is an offensive term used to show a lack of respect for a particular group of people. Intentional use of a slur can be classified as hate speech and contributes to the oppression of the related minoritized group.

Even if the slur is used against people outside of the affected group or as a "joke", it still has harm by reinforcing harmful opressive norms. This ignorance is simply not acceptable in this era and it is most definitely not funny. It's honestly so embarassing that someone feels the need to use slurs in order to be funny :/

Read More: Words that Wound Anthology, Just Words: On Speech and Hidden Harm by Mary Kate McGowan

Doesn't try to get to know your friends or talks shit about them

Friends are part of your support system, and it's normal for a long term partner to get to know, or even join your friend group. Similar to not taking an interest in your interests, intentionally not getting to know your friends is reflective of wanting you to integrate into their life, but putting no effort in doing the same for you.

Talking shit about your friends is a tool that is sometimes used as a way to isolate you from your support system. By alienating you from people who would likely call your partner out on their bs, your partner is trying to make it as hard as possible for you to leave them. Isolation is often seen as a warning sign of potenital future or current abuse. If you or someone you know is in an abusive situation, please refer to our Resource Page for help.

You can read more about isolation as a manipulation tactic here.

Whines, guilt-trips, or mopes around when you don't have sex with them

Whining and moping around when you simply say "no" is a form of manipulation with the goal of coercing you into sex. While your partner may not see it this way, this action is ignoring your consent and bodily autonomy. Consent is a sober & enthusiastic yes, and having to convince you to have sex with them is not enthusiastic! It's always been weird to me that people want to have sex with people who aren't in the mood at all ... like how is that fun ?

This tiktok explains this concept really well. And this one! This tiktok explains a common experience of straight women and it sucks so much !

Pressures you to have sex without protection

This is another form of your partner ignoring your consent and bodily autonomy. Forcing you to not use protection is a form of sexual assualt. Any form of coercion (ie threatening to break up with you unless you have unprotected sex, etc.) is also considered sexual assault.

You can read more about sexual coercion here.

Addicted to drugs or alcohol

Addiction can often be unnoticed, especially in college. Things like drinking almost every night or needing weed to sleep or eat are often normalized among young adults, but these habits are reflective of a dependance on a substance in order to function. While addiction is a disease and deserves to be treated with compassion, dating someone with an addiction that they refuse to aknowledge can be mentally taxing. It's very difficult to have an equal partnership with someone who has a serious drug or alcohol dependency.

You can read more about dating in addiction here.

Mentally ill AND has terrible coping mechanisms

Mentall illness is an incredibly difficult thing to navigate, but it is important to remember that is is not your job to regulate and manage someone else's emotions or mental illness. You are also not responsible for being someone's sole support system. Your partner putting all of that on you is an example of unhealthy coping. If your partner is struggling with this, encourage them to expand their support system and find other methods for regulating their emotions (see link for more info on this). While it is normal to want to support your partner through difficult times, this should not be at the expense of your own mental wellbeing.

If someone is using their mentall illness as an excuse for harming you or others, RUN!! Mentall illness is an explanation for behavior, not an excuse and it is important for individuals struggling with mental illness to take accountability for their actions and apologize without using their diagnosis as way to avoid fault.

You can read more about dating with mental illness and healthy coping mechanisms here.

Tells you that "you're making too big of a deal about this" or that you're crazy for something that is valid

This is an example of gaslighting. Gaslighting is a harmful manipulation tactic used to get someone to question their sanity or reasoning. Gaslighting is often used to take something that is their fault (like cheating, "I never did that, you're crazy") and turn it on you ("why dont you trust me?!").

You can read more about gaslighting here. Gaslighting is often seen in abusive relationships or can be a warning sign for abuse; see our Resources page for more information.

Multiple of your friends hate your partner

Oftentimes, people in relationships can ignore the red flags because they have rose colored glasses on. However, you friends do not have those glasses. They can see the impact that your partner has on your happiness and wellbeing, and if it's not positive, a good friend will let you know! They're not crazy or jealous or whatever else your partner might say about them, they see through your partner's bs and are trying to protect you!

You can read more about seeing your friend in a bad relationship here.

You've caught them in a lie multiple times

Honesty is part of the foundation of any healthy relationship (not the brutal "I'm just being honest why are you offended" honesty - that's a red flag, but just not lying). Honesty means telling your partner when something is bothering you, and them doing the same. It means not lying about where they are or what they've been doing. It also means admitting when you might've fucked up, taking accountability, and doing what you can to earn forgiveness.

You can read more about honesty in relationships here.

They take no interest in the things you're interested in.

Tbh I see this one most often in heterosexual relationships. While it can happen in any relationship. I often see men having the expectation of their partner integrating seamlessly into their lives (they don't want to be inconvenienced at all), but they often do not return the favor. I love hearing people talk about things they're passionate about or teaching me about their hobbies, and want the same thing in return from a partner. Your partner doesn't have to love everything that you love, but they should actively listen and take interest when you talk about it.

You don't want to be more like them

The best relationships are the ones where both or all partners are making eachother better people. A relationship where you don't grow positively at all seems like a waste. Why commit your life to this person if they're not making you better in some shape or form? Also, if you look at someone and don't see anything you want to be more of, then you're basically training this person to get on your level - all of the emotional labor is on your side.

Punches holes in walls

The scariest example of punching walls is when someone is fighting with their partner. When they punch a wall out of anger, they are showing you what they are capable of doing to you. It is an active threat and reflective of possible physical abuse in the future. Alternatively, punching walls when their sport team loses or when they're generally frusterated shows a general lack of maturity. They need to learn how to feel things other than anger before they get into a relationship.

Punching holes can be a warning sign of abuse. See our Resources page for more information and support.

They're super messy / never clean shared spaces / their roommate(s) don't like them

Serious relationship often progress to living together and it is important to take note of their behaviors in a household. Women notoriously do disproportionally more unpaid household labor and that can be exhausting and lead to growing resentment over time. Knowing how to clean is a basic adult skill that all people should have! Unless you want to be the only one cleaning for eternity, this is definitely a red flag.

You can read more about shared unpaid labor here and here.

Rude to waiters or don't tip well

Being rude to service staff or not tipping well is often reflective of classism. Classism is the belief that someone's economic status makes them more valuable or worthy in society. Class mobility is getting increasingly harder in the United States and the "pulling yourself up by the bootstraps" mentality is super outdated and unrealistic at this point. Also ... service workers are literally the backbone of our society and it's so wack to disparage a service that they rely on to make their life easier!
